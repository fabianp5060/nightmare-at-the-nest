# Meet Packet Requirements
## Highland High School 1st Track & Field Invitational — "Nightmare at the Nest"

---

## Project Context

**Event Name:** Nightmare at the Nest
**Host School:** Highland High School
**Edition:** 1st Annual (Inaugural)
**Theme:** The name "Nightmare at the Nest" implies a Halloween/spooky or intensity-themed branding. Highland's mascot is the Hawk, so "the Nest" is the Hawks' home. The "Nightmare" component suggests a meet so competitive and challenging it strikes fear into competitors — a bold statement for a first-year meet.

**Purpose of This Document:** This requirements file defines every section, data point, and design consideration needed to produce the official meet packet for the Nightmare at the Nest invitational. It is intended to be comprehensive enough that any person picking up this project mid-stream can understand exactly what is needed and what decisions remain open.

**Reference Documents Analyzed:**
- 2026 Thunder Invite Meet Packet (Desert Vista High School, Phoenix AZ) — Full-service invitational, state-qualifying, 15 teams, running and field events
- 2026 Field of Dreams (Red Mountain High School) — Inaugural field-event-only invitational with elite/seeded session format
- 2026 Aztec Invite Information Packet (Corona del Sol High School, Tempe AZ) — 20th Annual, state-qualifying, co-ed, comprehensive running and field events

## Section 1: Cover Page / Header

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
The cover page is the first impression of the meet. For a first-year themed meet, it should immediately communicate the brand, establish credibility, and make coaches feel excited to attend.

### Required Data Points
- Meet name in large, prominent text: **Nightmare at the Nest**
- Hosted by: Highland High School
- "1st Annual" designation (or "Inaugural") — important for setting legacy expectations
- Full date of the meet (day of week, month, day, year) — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Event type label: "Track and Field Invitational"
- School address / venue address
- School logo or mascot graphic (Highland Hawk)
- Theme graphic / visual element consistent with the "Nightmare" branding

### Optional / Nice to Have
- Tagline or motto for the meet (e.g., "Where Legends Are Made" or a spooky-themed line)
- Brief line welcoming coaches and athletes
- Whether the meet is state-qualifying (if confirmed, this is a major draw and should appear on the cover)

### Design Notes
- The cover should feel premium. A first-year meet needs to inspire confidence that it is well-organized.
- Use the school's official colors as the primary palette.
- The "Nightmare" theme can be incorporated through font choice, graphic elements (feathers, talons, dark dramatic imagery), and color accents — without being so Halloween-specific that it dates poorly.
- Reference: Thunder Invite uses a simple, clean header. Aztec Invite uses a formal letterhead-style header. For a branded themed meet, aim for something bolder.

## Section 4: Schedule of Events

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
The schedule is the operational spine of the packet. Coaches use it to plan athlete warm-up timing, report calls, and conflicts between events. It must be clear, comprehensive, and well-formatted.

### Required Data Points

#### Pre-Meet Timeline
- Gate open time — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Weigh-in times for throws (javelin separately, shot/discus separately) — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Warm-up period for field events with start times — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Coaches meeting time and location — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- First event start time — **[DECISION NEEDED]**

#### Field Events Schedule (each event needs)
- Warm-up / run-through start time
- Competition start time
- Approximate flight duration (helps coaches plan conflicts)
- Order of genders (which goes first, boys or girls, or simultaneous)
- For pole vault and high jump: opening heights — **[DECISION NEEDED]**

#### Running Events Order
Standard AZ invitational running order (customize as needed):
1. 3200m (or 4x800 relay if included)
2. 100m/110m Hurdles (girls/boys)
3. 100m Dash
4. 1600m Run
5. 4x100m Relay
6. 400m Dash
7. 300m Hurdles
8. 800m Run
9. 200m Dash
10. 3200m Run (if not run first)
11. 4x400m Relay

Note: Aztec runs Girls 3200m first in the a.m. as the first running event — this is an AZ-common format. Thunder runs boys track races first, then girls. Confirm preference. — **[DECISION NEEDED]**

#### Rolling Schedule Note
All three reference packets use rolling schedules. The packet must state: "We will keep a rolling schedule. Please ensure your athletes listen to report calls."

### Format Recommendations
- Present the schedule in a two-column table if both field and running events are occurring simultaneously (field on left, running on right — see Thunder Invite and Field of Dreams models).
- Include a disclaimer: "Schedule is tentative and subject to change. Final schedule will be released closer to the meet."
- Aztec Invite explicitly states: "Events may start up to 10 minutes earlier than scheduled" — include this.

### Nice to Have

- Note about when awards ceremony will be held

## Section 5: Entry Information & Deadlines

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
Coaches need clear instructions on how and when to submit entries. Ambiguity here causes administrative headaches on meet day.

### Required Data Points
- Entry platform / system to be used (Athletic.net is the Arizona standard) — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Entry deadline: specific date and time (e.g., "Thursday, [date] at 11:59 PM") — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Number of individual entries allowed per event per school — **[DECISION NEEDED]** (standard: 4 or 5)
- Number of relay entries allowed per event per school (standard: 1)
- Instructions on what mark to use for entry (seed time/mark):
  - Use current season FAT marks when available
  - Use prior season marks if no current mark exists
  - Use hand-timed trial marks as last resort
  - Do not exaggerate marks — all three packets emphasize this point
- Instructions on athlete name accuracy (Aztec specifically calls out: "triple-check names are spelled correctly in Athletic.net, MileSplit, and MaxPreps")
- Policy on no-marks / unverified marks (if space allows, or fill order)
- Substitution policy after deadline:
  - Are substitutions allowed? — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
  - If yes: what is the process? (e.g., change form at coaches meeting, same heat/lane)
  - All three packets handle this differently — a clear policy must be chosen
- Scratch policy (athletes who cannot compete after entry is confirmed)
- Statement that if an athlete is entered they are expected to compete (Field of Dreams is emphatic about this)

### Nice to Have
- Link or URL to the entry platform
- Screenshot or diagram of how to enter on the platform
- Note about heat sheet release timeline (e.g., "heat sheets will be available by [day before meet]")

### Design Notes
- This section is often where coaches spend the most time. Use bullet points, not paragraphs.
- Bold the deadline date and time.
- Field of Dreams goes the furthest on entry integrity: "Do not enter an athlete you 'think' will compete. Enter athletes who will compete!" — a version of this statement is recommended.

## Section 8: Rules & Regulations (General)

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
Establishes the official ruleset and any meet-specific enforcement priorities. Officials need this. Coaches need to prep their athletes. All three reference packets reference NFHS rules as the governing framework.

### Required Data Points
- Governing rules statement: "Official rules will follow NFHS rules."
- Uniform rules enforcement — **[DECISION NEEDED]** (NFHS uniform compliance is standard)
- Electronic device policy:
  - Athletes may not have electronic devices on or inside the track / competition area
  - Consequence: potential disqualification of the offending athlete and/or teammates (Field of Dreams and Aztec both include this)
  - "This is at the discretion of the Head Referee or Event Judge"
  - Competition areas will be denoted by flagging
- Spike rule:
  - "Athletes are only allowed to have 1/4-inch pyramid spikes" — standard AZ rule
  - Athletes caught with longer spikes: DQ from the event
  - Exception for javelin: cleats or 1/2" spikes recommended on grass — **[DECISION NEEDED if javelin is on grass]**
  - Field of Dreams does a spike check and marks approved athletes — consider implementing this
- No reverse runs / non-legal direction warm-up rule on horizontal jumps, pole vault, and javelin runways:
  - "THIS WILL BE STRICTLY ENFORCED!" — consistent language in all three packets; replicate the emphasis
- Coaches box rules:
  - One coach per gender allowed in coaches box at each field event
  - Coaches must check in with the head event judge
  - No coach allowed on or inside the track during running events
  - Pole vault coaches box: must be district-approved coach (Field of Dreams includes this; consider it)
- Infield access: closed to spectators, used for staging and warm-up only
- Food and beverage policy on infield (if turf): no food, no non-water beverages — **[DECISION NEEDED based on surface type]**
- Jury of Appeals / Games Committee:
  - Define who comprises the games committee — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
  - Field of Dreams: "Jury of Appeals will consist of the head coaches of each team present at the time of request" — this is a clean, fair structure for a first-year meet

### Nice to Have
- Statement about behavior standards for coaches and spectators
- Statement about consequences for unsportsmanlike conduct

## Section 9: Event-Specific Rules

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
Each event type has its own procedural rules. Athletes and coaches need these details before meet day to prepare properly.

### 9A: Running Events

**Required Data Points:**
- All track events run as finals (heats against time), multiple sections as needed — confirm format — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Lane races (100m, 200m, 400m, hurdles): start and finish in same lanes
- Distance events start format:
  - 800m: box-and-alley start (standard)
  - 1600m and 3200m: waterfall start (single or double based on entries) — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Boys track races order vs. girls: which goes first — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
  - Thunder: boys first, then girls
  - Aztec: girls first ("GIRLS will participate first")
- Athlete check-in: clerk of course, located in middle of field/infield — specify exact location — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Clerk of course note: athletes who fail to check in will be scratched
- 4x100m Relay: utilizes NFHS 30m exchange zones (updated rule, worth noting explicitly)
- 4x400m Relay: 3-turn stagger (standard)
- All 4 relay athletes must report to clerking AND the starting line

**Nice to Have:**
- Note about report call procedure (PA announcements, number of calls before scratch)

### 9B: Throwing Events (Shot Put, Discus, Javelin)

**Required Data Points:**
- Weigh-in times and location — **[DECISION NEEDED]**

- Implement ownership policy: athletes encouraged to bring own implements
- After weigh-in, implements become "mutual property" available to all competitors (Aztec model — decide if adopting this)
- School name must be marked on all implements
- Host school not responsible for lost or damaged implements
- All throws format: 3 prelim throws, top 9 advance to finals for 3 more throws — **[DECISION NEEDED on cutoff number]**
- Shot put and discus location: separate from main track (southwest or other area) — specify — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Javelin: conducted on grass — cleats or 1/2" spikes recommended — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Javelin: no warm-up without official or coach present

- Shot and discus: host provides implements — **[DECISION NEEDED]**

## Section 13: Trainer / Sports Medicine / First Aid

**Priority: Required**

### Purpose
Every invitational packet should address medical support so coaches know what to expect and can prepare accordingly.

### Required Data Points
- Whether a certified athletic trainer will be on site — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
- Location of first aid / training station — **[DECISION NEEDED]**
  - Aztec: near finish line, south end of stadium
  - Thunder: on the infield
  - Field of Dreams: northeast end of the field
- Scope of services the trainer will provide. Standard list (based on Thunder Invite Exhibit A model):
  - Ice bags for acute (same-day) injuries
  - Taping for athletes who bring their own supplies (tape, pre-wrap, heel pads)
  - Wound care / first aid for injuries during the meet
  - Water/Gatorade stations (if host provides hydration)
  - Light recovery tools (foam rollers, percussion massagers)
  - Heat illness management (shade, ice tub)
- Scope of services NOT provided. Standard exclusions (based on Thunder Invite):
  - Sunscreen
  - Replacement spikes or spike wrenches
  - Ice for personal bottles (only for acute injuries)
  - Elastic wraps for pre-/post-race chronic injuries
  - Heat balm / Icy Hot
  - Tape for jump/relay marks
- Special medical notes:
  - Athletes with allergies or asthma: bring all required inhalers and medications
  - Diabetic athletes: bring snacks and drinks to manage blood sugar
  - Emergency transport forms: coaches should have transport authorization for all athletes (in case of ambulance transport)

### Nice to Have

- Clear distinction between acute and chronic injuries for ice provision

### Design Notes

## Section 20: Tone & Branding Guidance

### Meet Identity

**Name:** Nightmare at the Nest
**Host:** Highland Hawks
**Year:** Inaugural / 1st Annual

The name "Nightmare at the Nest" creates an immediate expectation: this is a meet that will be intense, competitive, and memorable. The host school is making a statement that their home turf is a place where visiting teams face a formidable challenge. This is aspirational branding for year one — the packet must deliver on it.

### Tone Guidelines

**Confident, not apologetic.** First-year meets sometimes over-explain or over-apologize for being new. Do not do this. Write the packet as if this meet has always been prestigious and always will be.

**Welcoming, but firm.** The rules sections should be clear and non-negotiable. Use language like "THIS WILL BE STRICTLY ENFORCED" where appropriate (all three reference packets do this for the no-reverse-run rule). At the same time, the welcome section and hospitality sections should feel warm and inviting.

**Themed without being campy.** The horror/nightmare theme can appear in:
- The cover graphic and design
- Section headers (optional — e.g., "The Nest" instead of "Venue Information")
- Award names or descriptions
- Any exhibition event names
It should NOT appear in rules sections, schedule, or anywhere that requires maximum clarity.

**Professional.** The packet reflects the host school's competence. Clean formatting, no typos, consistent font use, proper grammar. A poorly formatted packet signals a poorly run meet.

### Color Palette

**[DECISION NEEDED]** — Confirm Highland High School's official colors. Most AZ high schools use standard school colors. The "Nightmare" theme might warrant adding a dark accent (black, deep navy, or crimson) alongside the school's primary colors.

### Typography

- Headlines: Bold, large, school-primary color
- Body text: Clean, readable sans-serif or serif, black or very dark gray
- Emphasis: Bold or all-caps for critical rules (as used in all three reference packets)
- Avoid decorative or novelty fonts in rules/schedule sections — only use them on the cover

### Logo / Mascot Usage

- Use the Highland Hawk mascot on the cover page
- Consider a custom "Nightmare at the Nest" meet logo if resources allow (even a simple graphic treatment of the name)
- All pages should have a consistent header or footer with the meet name

### Design Recommendations

- **Cover page:** Full bleed or bordered design with meet name, date, hawk imagery, and theme elements
- **Interior pages:** Clean two-column or single-column layout with clear section headers
- **Tables:** Use for schedule and invoice — align columns precisely
- **Maps:** Print at full page (8.5x11) for readability
- **Length:** Aim for 6–10 pages total; reference packets range from 3–6 pages (not counting exhibits)
- **Format:** Design as a PDF for digital distribution and as a printable document for physical copies at the coaches meeting
