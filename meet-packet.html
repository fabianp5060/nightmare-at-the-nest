Welcome & Meet Overview

Welcome coaches, athletes, families, and officials to the inaugural Nightmare at the Nest Invitational, hosted by Highland High School Track & Field. This is an AIA state-qualifying meet, and we are excited to begin what we expect will be an annual tradition of fierce, well-run competition at the Nest. We are proud to open our facility to some of the best programs in the East Valley and beyond.

Please read this packet thoroughly. All logistical, entry, and competition information is contained herein. This document will be updated as details are finalized — check Athletic.net for the most current version. Questions may be directed to Meet Director Jacob Rutter at the contact information below.

Competition Rules & Format

Clerk of Course / Running Check-In: North end of the infield. Athletes must report to the clerk when called. Failure to report may result in scratching.

General Rules

General Conduct
  • Meet conducted under current NFHS rules and AIA guidelines.
  • Spike Rule: 1/4″ pyramid spikes or less, strictly enforced on all surfaces. Javelin athletes on grass may use longer spikes at their discretion.
  • Coaches are restricted to designated coaches boxes during competition.
  • Infield is closed to spectators at all times.
NO REVERSE RUNNING on the track or in throwing/jumping areas — STRICTLY ENFORCED
Running Events
  • All running events contested as finals (heats against time).
  • Girls compete first in all events.
  • 800m: Box-alley starts.
  • 1600m / 3200m: Single or double waterfall start depending on the number of entries. Game management may place athletes in boxes at the start.
  • Athletes must check in with the Clerk of Course (north end of infield) when called. Failure to check in may result in scratching.
  • Relay teams: All 4 athletes must be present at the clerk of course AND at the starting line.

Throwing Events (Shot Put, Discus, Javelin)

Format & Flights
  • Format: 3 preliminary attempts | Top 9 athletes advance to finals.
  • 15 minutes between flights.
  • Finals begin immediately after the last prelim flight.
  • Javelin is contested on the grass fields east of the stadium.
  • Athletes who leave the throwing area to compete in a running event have 10 minutes to return.
Implements & Weigh-In
  • All implements must be marked with school name. The host is not responsible for lost or damaged implements.
  • Implements and weigh-ins will be checked underneath the visitor's bleachers on the east side of the stadium.

Long Jump & Triple Jump

Long Jump
  • Format: 3 preliminary attempts | Top 9 advance to finals.
  • Takeoff board: 8 feet.
  • Gender order: Girls first, then Boys.
  • Long Jump completes before Triple Jump begins.
  • Per NFHS rules, no run backs are allowed.
Triple Jump
  • Format: 3 preliminary attempts | Top 9 advance to finals.
  • Takeoff boards: 24 ft / 28 ft / 32 ft.
  • Gender order: Boys first, then Girls.
  • Begins after Long Jump competition concludes.
  • Per NFHS rules, no run backs are allowed.

High Jump & Pole Vault

High Jump
  • Girls opening height: 4′0″
  • Boys opening height: 5′2″
  • Bar increments: 2″ per height throughout.
  • Gender order: Girls first, then Boys.
  • Per NFHS rules, no run backs are allowed.
  • An athlete can be skipped when it's their turn, and jump at that height when they return, but a height will not be held for an athlete attending other events.
Pole Vault
  • Girls opening height: 6′0″
  • Boys opening height: 7′6″
  • Bar increments: 6″ per height throughout.
  • Gender order: Girls first, then Boys.
  • Per NFHS rules, no run backs are allowed.
  • An athlete can be skipped when it's their turn, and jump at that height when they return, but a height will not be held for an athlete attending other events.

Parking & Transportation

Spectator Parking
Main school parking lot off Highland Hawks Dr — opens at 3:30 PM. Enter via the Main Entrance on the south side of the lot.
Team Bus Drop-Off
Buses drop athletes on N. Roadrunner Drive (east side of campus). After drop-off, buses must leave campus and may park at Highland Junior High School (HJHS) or return at the conclusion of the meet.
